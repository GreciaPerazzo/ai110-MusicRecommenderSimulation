# Reflection on User Profile Comparisons

## High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi

The High-Energy Pop profile (pop genre, happy mood, high energy/valence) produced recommendations like "Sunrise City" and "Gym Hero" at the top, with strong genre and mood bonuses pushing pop tracks forward. In contrast, the Chill Lofi profile (lofi genre, chill mood, low energy) favored "Library Rain" and "Midnight Coding," emphasizing low-energy, relaxed vibes with perfect matches.

What changed: The top songs shifted from upbeat pop anthems to calm lofi beats, with scores reflecting energy differences (high vs. low) and genre switches. This makes sense because the profiles have opposite energy preferences and genres, so the system correctly adapts to match the user's "vibe"—high-energy for excitement, low-energy for relaxation—demonstrating the recommender's sensitivity to numeric and categorical attributes.

## Deep Intense Rock vs Conflicted

The Deep Intense Rock profile (rock genre, intense mood, high energy/low valence) topped with "Storm Runner," benefiting from full bonuses for rock/intense matches. The Conflicted profile (pop genre, sad mood, high energy/low valence) still ranked "Gym Hero" and "Sunrise City" highly due to the pop genre bonus, despite the sad mood mismatch.

What changed: Rock recommendations focused on intense, high-energy tracks, while the conflicted profile mixed pop with some sad-themed songs like "Heartbreak Blues," but genre loyalty dominated. This makes sense because the rock profile gets bonuses for matching both genre and mood, creating a cohesive "intense rock" list, whereas the conflicted profile's genre bonus overrides mood conflicts, revealing how categorical matches can overpower nuanced preferences like low valence or sad moods.</content>
